GeoQuant — Geopolitical Risk & Sector Rotation Intelligence SystemOverviewGeoQuant is a quantitative financial modeling framework designed to anticipate market risk regimes and forecast sector leadership probabilities. By integrating macroeconomic risk signals (such as Oil, Gold, and VIX) with equity volatility modeling, the system provides a robust, data-driven approach to portfolio intelligence.The framework successfully bridges traditional financial econometrics with modern machine learning by integrating:Cross-asset risk monitoringVolatility regime detectionGARCH-based conditional variance forecastingMachine learning classificationSector rotation probabilistic scoringSystem Architecture1. Risk Monitoring EngineThe foundation of the system's risk assessment, focusing on modeling and forecasting market turbulence.Log return modeling20-day rolling volatility estimationGARCH(1,1) conditional variance forecastingRandom Forest regime classifier2. Sector Rotation ModelA predictive engine that ranks and scores market sectors based on momentum, relative strength, and risk-adjusted metrics.20-day momentum factorRelative strength vs. S&P 500Rolling volatility risk penaltyCross-sectional z-score normalizationSoftmax probability transformation with temperature scalingMathematical FrameworkThe system's calculations are grounded in the following mathematical models:Log Return$$r_t = \ln\left(\frac{P_t}{P_{t-1}}\right)$$Rolling Volatility$$\sigma_t = \sqrt{\frac{1}{n} \sum_{i=1}^n r_{t-i}^2}$$GARCH(1,1)$$\sigma_t^2 = \omega + \alpha r_{t-1}^2 + \beta \sigma_{t-1}^2$$Sector Score$$Score_i = 0.5 Z_i^{Momentum} + 0.4 Z_i^{RelativeStrength} + 0.1 Z_i^{Volatility}$$Softmax Probability$$P_i = \frac{\exp\left(\frac{Score_i - \max(Score)}{T}\right)}{\sum_j \exp\left(\frac{Score_j - \max(Score)}{T}\right)}$$Technologies UsedLanguage: PythonWeb Framework: StreamlitMachine Learning: Scikit-LearnData Processing: NumPy, PandasFinancial Data: yFinanceEconometrics: Arch (GARCH modeling)Visualization: PlotlyDeploymentThe application is live and deployed using Streamlit Cloud.AcknowledgmentsThe development, mathematical formatting, and documentation of this system were accelerated and refined with the assistance of Large Language Models, including OpenAI's ChatGPT and Google's Gemini.AuthorSamyak Baid
